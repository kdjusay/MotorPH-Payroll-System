Introduction

1. Introduction

Welcome to the User Guide for the MotorPH Payroll System! This section serves as an introductory overview of our comprehensive payroll management solution. Whether you're an employee, a member of the HR department, part of the finance team, or an IT professional, this guide will help you navigate and utilize the system effectively to streamline payroll processes and enhance organizational efficiency.

1.1. User Roles

Employee:

- Log in to the system securely.

- Submit leave and overtime requests.

- View and download payslips.

HR Department:

- Approve or reject leave and overtime requests.

- Access and update employee information.

- View and download payslip history for employees.

Finance Department:

- Process payroll for employees.

- Generate payroll reports for financial analysis and auditing purposes.

IT Department:

- Process payroll for employees.

- Access login audit reports to monitor system usage, employees' entry time, and security.

2. Getting Started

This section outlines the fundamental requirements for using the MotorPH Payroll System and provides instructions on how users can access the system.

2.1. Hardware Prerequisites

Users will need a computer or laptop with internet access, ensuring they meet the system's minimum requirements for smooth operation. Ensuring that the device has sufficient processing power, memory, and storage space to handle the system efficiently.

2.2. Software Prerequisites

Before accessing the MotorPH Payroll System, users must ensure that their devices have the necessary software components installed. These prerequisites include:

- Users should have a modern web browser installed on their computer or laptop. Compatible browsers may include Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, or Safari.

- Users should have access to a high-speed internet connection to ensure smooth operation and timely data retrieval.

- Ensure that your operating system is up-to-date to maintain compatibility with the system's requirements.

2.3. Accessing the System

Once users have ensured that their devices meet the necessary software prerequisites, they can access the MotorPH Payroll System by following these steps:

1. Launch the web browser installed on your computer or laptop.

2. Enter the URL provided by your system administrator or IT department into the address bar of the web browser.

3. On the login page, enter your username and password.

4. Once you have entered your login credentials, choose which department you are in and click on the "Login" button to access the system.

5. Upon successful login, users will be directed to the system dashboard or main interface, where they can access various features and functionalities based on their user role and permissions.

3. Using The Payroll System

3.1. Secure Login Procedures

1. Open the payroll system web link or a dedicated application, users will be directed to the login page.

2. Users will log in using their unique username and password combination.

3. Choose what department you are in.

4. The system will verify the entered credentials against the stored user data to authenticate the user's identity.

5. Upon accessing the payroll system, users will be directed to the main page.

3.2. Salary and Deduction Calculations

Begin by inputting the employee's ID number into the designated field. This unique identifier ensures accurate tracking of the employee's salary and deductions.

Calculate the employee's basic salary based on predetermined factors such as:

  • Monthly wage or hourly rate
  • Number of hours worked (for hourly employees)
  • Allowances
  • Deduction
  • Overtime
  • Gross Pay

Subtract total deductions from the employee's gross salary to obtain the net salary amount.

Net Salary = Gross Salary - Total Deductions

Verification and Finalization:

  • Review the calculated salary and deductions to ensure accuracy and compliance with company policies and regulations.
  • Provide a summary of the salary breakdown, including gross salary, deductions, and net salary, for transparency and clarity.
  • Finalize the salary calculation process and generate relevant documents, such as pay slips.

Recordkeeping and Documentation:

  • Maintain accurate records of salary calculations, deductions, and payment details for each employee.
  • Store documentation securely and in compliance with data protection regulations to ensure confidentiality and privacy.
  • Update salary records regularly to reflect any changes in employee compensation or deductions.

Continuous Monitoring and Adjustment:

  • Monitor salary and deduction calculations regularly to identify any discrepancies or errors.
  • Adjust calculations as needed to reflect changes in employee status, compensation rates, or regulatory requirements.
  • Communicate any updates or adjustments to employees in a timely and transparent manner.

3.3. Leave Applications

    1. Navigate to the "Leave Applications" section of the payroll system.

    2. Users will access a leave dashboard displaying their leave balances, history, and options to apply for leave.

    3. Employees will select the type of leave they wish to apply for, such as vacation, sick leave, or personal leave.

    4. Users will fill out a leave application form specifying the dates of leave, the type of leave, and any additional notes or attachments if required.

    5. The leave application will be sent to the designated approver, typically the HR Department, for review and approval.
